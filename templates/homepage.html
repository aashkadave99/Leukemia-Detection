Leukemia is a cancer of the blood cells. There are several broad categories of blood cells, including red blood cells (RBCs), white blood cells (WBCs), and platelets.
Generally, leukemia refers to cancers of the WBCs.
WBCs are a vital part of your immune system.
They protect your body from invasion by bacteria, viruses, and fungi, as well as from abnormal cells and other foreign substances.

Leukemia begins in a cell in the bone marrow.
The cell undergoes a change and becomes a type of leukemia cell.
Once the marrow cell undergoes a leukemic change, the leukemia cells may grow and survive better than normal cells.
Over time, the leukemia cells crowd out or suppress the development of normal cells
